# Chapter 4: Appetizers, Salads and Side Dishes

4.1 Corn and Black Bean Salad

Serves: 6  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Total Time: 15 minutes

Description

A fresh, tangy salad loaded with corn, black beans, peppers, and lime juice. Great on its own, as a dip with chips, or spooned over tacos and grilled meats.

Ingredients

  • 1 (15 oz / 425 g) can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1½ cups (250 g) cooked corn kernels (fresh or frozen and thawed)
  • ½ red bell pepper, diced
  • ½ green bell pepper, diced
  • ¼ cup (30 g) finely chopped red onion
  • 1–2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

    • Instructions

    1. In a large bowl, combine the beans, corn, peppers, onion, and cilantro.
    2. Drizzle with lime juice and olive oil, then toss to combine.
    3. Season with salt and pepper. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

    Notes

  • Add diced avocado just before serving for extra richness.
  • Can be made 1 day ahead; store covered in the fridge.

    • 4.2 Knorr Spinach Dip

    Serves: 8  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Chill Time: 2 hours  Total Time: 2 hours 15 minutes

    Description

    A creamy, nostalgic party dip packed with spinach and savory vegetable seasoning. Serve with bread, crackers, or fresh vegetables.

    Ingredients

  • 1 box (10 oz / 284 g) frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • *or* 1 bunch fresh spinach (10 oz / 284 g), blanched and squeezed dry
  • 1 container (16 oz / 454 g) sour cream
  • 1 cup (240 ml) mayonnaise (see recipe)
  • 1 package (1.4 oz / 40 g) Knorr Vegetable Recipe Mix
  • 1 can (8 oz / 227 g) water chestnuts, drained and chopped
  • 3 green onions, chopped

    • Instructions

    1. Prepare spinach: thaw frozen spinach and squeeze out excess moisture using a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth, *or* blanch fresh spinach in boiling water for 30 seconds, transfer to an ice bath, drain, and squeeze dry.
    2. In a large bowl, mix sour cream, mayonnaise, and Knorr mix.
    3. Stir in spinach, water chestnuts, and green onions until evenly combined.
    4. Chill for at least 2 hours to allow flavors to meld. Serve cold.

    Notes

  • Serve in a hollowed round loaf of bread, such as pumpernickel or rye.
  • Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.

    • 4.3 Herbed Oyster Crackers

    Yield: About 6 cups  Prep Time: 5 minutes  Total Time: 5 minutes

    Description

    A retro, addictively seasoned snack made with oyster crackers tossed in ranch, dill, lemon pepper, and just a touch of heat. Great for parties, road trips, or munching straight from the jar.

    Ingredients

  • 1 (12 oz / 340 g) package oyster crackers
  • 1 cup (240 ml) neutral oil (e.g., canola or vegetable)
  • 1 packet (1 oz / 28 g) Hidden Valley Ranch seasoning mix
  • ½ tsp dried dill
  • ½ tsp lemon pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp onion powder
  • 2–3 drops Tabasco sauce

    • Instructions

    1. In a large bowl, whisk together the oil, ranch seasoning, dill, lemon pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and Tabasco.
    2. Add the oyster crackers and toss gently until evenly coated.
    3. Let sit for 10–15 minutes, tossing occasionally to allow flavors to absorb.
    4. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week.

    Notes

  • For a lighter version, reduce oil to ¾ cup.
  • Also works with mini saltines or snack-size pretzels.
  • Let sit overnight for best flavor saturation.

    • 4.4 Dill Pickles

    Yield: 2 quarts (about 8 servings)  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Chill Time: 1 week  Total Time: 1 week 15 minutes

    Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900 g) Persian, baby or pickling cucumbers, cut into spears as needed to fit the jars
  • 4 garlic cloves, halved (2 tbsp / 20 g)
  • 2 tsp (8 g) mustard seeds
  • 2 tsp (8 g) peppercorns
  • Small bunch dill sprigs
  • 2 cups (480 ml) water
  • 2 cups (480 ml) white vinegar
  • ¼ cup (50 g) sugar
  • 2 tbsp (30 g) sea salt

    • Instructions

    1. Boil vinegar, water, sugar, and salt until fully dissolved.
    2. Pack cucumbers, garlic, mustard seeds, peppercorns, and dill into clean wide-mouth mason jars.
    3. Pour hot brine over contents, cool to room temperature, and refrigerate for 1 week.

    Notes

  • Pickles are ready when they lose their raw snap and take on a slightly translucent appearance.
  • Store in refrigerator for up to 1 month.
  • Use firm, fresh cucumbers for best texture.

    • 4.5 Spinach Bars

    Serves: 12–16  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Bake Time: 35 minutes  Total Time: 50 minutes

    Description

    Savory, cheesy spinach bars loaded with sharp cheddar, mushrooms, and onion. Great for gatherings, snack tables, or brunch spreads. Serve hot or at room temperature.

    Ingredients

  • 1 (10 oz / 284 g) package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and well drained
  • 4 tbsp (57 g) unsalted butter
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup (240 ml) milk
  • ¾ cup (90 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 12–16 oz (340–450 g) sharp cheddar cheese, finely grated
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • ½ cup (50 g) chopped mushrooms

    • Instructions

    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Place butter in a 9×13-inch (23×33 cm) baking pan and place in oven to melt while it preheats.
    2. In a large bowl, mix together the eggs, milk, flour, salt, baking powder, spinach, cheese, onion, and mushrooms until evenly combined.
    3. Remove pan from oven once butter is melted. Pour spinach mixture into the hot pan and spread evenly.
    4. Bake for 35 minutes, or until lightly browned and set in the center.
    5. Let cool slightly, then cut into squares. Serve hot or at room temperature.

    Notes

  • For a spicier variation, add a pinch of cayenne or red pepper flakes.
  • Can be made ahead and reheated briefly before serving.

    • 4.6 Avocado Salmon Mousse

    Serves: 6  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Chill Time: 2 hours  Total Time: 2 hours 15 minutes

    Description

    This chilled mousse combines creamy avocado, flaked salmon, and lemon for a light and elegant appetizer. Serve with crackers, toast points, or cucumber slices for a retro-inspired starter.

    Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado (about 150 g), peeled and mashed
  • 1 (6 oz / 170 g) can boneless, skinless salmon, drained and flaked
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ¼ cup (60 ml) sour cream or full-fat yogurt
  • ¼ cup (60 ml) mayonnaise (see recipe)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: chopped fresh dill or parsley, for garnish

    • Instructions

    1. In a bowl, combine mashed avocado, salmon, lemon juice, Dijon, sour cream, and mayonnaise.
    2. Mix until smooth but still textured. Stir in garlic powder and season with salt and pepper.
    3. Transfer to a ramekin or small bowl. Cover and chill for at least 2 hours.
    4. Garnish with chopped herbs if using. Serve with crackers or vegetables.

    Notes

  • Can be made 1 day ahead.
  • Substitute fresh poached salmon if desired.

    • 4.10 Golden Gazpacho with Peaches and Tomatoes

    Serves: 6  Prep Time: 20 minutes  Chill Time: 2 hours  Total Time: 2 hours 20 minutes

    Description

    A refreshing chilled soup with sun gold tomatoes, peaches, and cucumbers.

    Ingredients

  • 2 pints sun gold cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup (150 g) red cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup (150 g) ripe yellow peaches, cubed
  • 1 cup (120 g) sliced English cucumber, peeled
  • ½ cup (60 g) orange bell pepper, chopped
  • ¼ cup (30 g) yellow onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tsp (12 g) kosher salt
  • ½ tsp (2 g) ground cumin
  • Cayenne pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) white wine or champagne vinegar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 cup (240 ml) cold water
  • Crumbled feta, diced peach, and sliced basil, for garnish

    • Instructions

    1. Blend tomatoes, peaches, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, garlic, salt, cumin, and cayenne until smooth.
    2. Add vinegar, lime juice, olive oil, and water; blend. Adjust seasoning.
    3. Chill for at least 2 hours or overnight.
    4. Serve with feta, peach cubes, and fresh basil.

    Notes

  • For an ultra-smooth texture, strain the soup through a fine-mesh sieve after blending.

    • 4.9 Artichoke Rice Salad

    Serves: 6–8  Prep Time: 15 minutes  Cook Time: 20 minutes  Chill Time: Overnight  Total Time: 12–16 hours

    Description

    A tangy, savory rice salad with marinated artichokes, olives, and a lightly spiced curry-mayo dressing. Serve chilled as a side, or bulk it up with shrimp or chicken for a complete main dish.

    Ingredients

  • 1 cup (200 g) long-grain white rice
  • 2½ cups (600 ml) chicken broth
  • 2 jars (6 oz / 170 g each) marinated artichoke hearts, drained (reserve marinade)
  • 8 green olives with pimiento, sliced
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • ½ green bell pepper, chopped
  • ¼ tsp curry powder (or more, to taste)
  • ⅓ cup (80 ml) mayonnaise (see recipe)
  • *Optional:* 1–2 cups (150–300 g) cooked shrimp or cubed chicken

    • Instructions

    1. Cook the rice in chicken broth according to package directions. Let cool completely.
    2. Once rice is cool, stir in green onions, green pepper, and sliced olives.
    3. In a small bowl, whisk together the reserved artichoke marinade, curry powder, and mayonnaise.
    4. Add the drained artichoke hearts and dressing to the rice mixture. Toss gently to combine.
    5. Chill overnight to allow flavors to develop.
    6. Before serving, taste and adjust seasoning — add more curry if desired.
    7. If using, stir in shrimp or cubed chicken just before serving.

    Notes

  • This salad holds well and improves after resting overnight.
  • Serve cold or at cool room temperature.
  • Makes a refreshing picnic or potluck dish with or without added protein.

    • 4.11 German-Style Warm Potato Salad

    Serves: 6–8  Prep Time: 20 minutes  Cook Time: 30 minutes  Total Time: 50 minutes

    Description

    A tangy potato salad with bacon, mustard, and vinegar. Serve warm for best flavor.

    Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (1 kg) red potatoes, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Salt, for boiling and seasoning
  • ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup (50 ml) apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp (20 g) sugar
  • 8 slices (250 g) bacon, chopped
  • ¼ cup (60 g) whole grain mustard
  • ¼ cup (15 g) fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp (3 g) black pepper
  • ½ tsp (3 g) salt

    • Instructions

    1. Rinse onion, then marinate in vinegar and sugar while you prepare the rest.
    2. Boil potatoes in salted water for 15–20 minutes until tender. Drain well.
    3. Cook bacon in a skillet until crisp.
    4. In a large bowl, combine warm potatoes with bacon and its rendered fat.
    5. Stir in mustard, parsley, salt, pepper, and drained onions.
    6. Toss gently and serve warm, garnished with extra parsley and pepper.

    Notes

  • Best erved warm.

    • 4.12 Broccoli Casserole

    Serves: 6  Prep Time: 10 minutes  Bake Time: 30 minutes  Total Time: 40 minutes

    Description

    A creamy, comforting broccoli casserole layered with mushroom soup, stuffing mix, and cheese. A nostalgic side dish that’s simple, hearty, and easy to prepare from pantry staples.

    Ingredients

  • 1 (16 oz / 454 g) package frozen broccoli spears
  • 1 can (10.5 oz / 298 g) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • ½ cup (120 ml) mayonnaise (see recipe)
  • ½ package (3 oz / 85 g) herb-seasoned stuffing mix
  • 1 cup (115 g) grated cheddar cheese *or* ½ cup (50 g) grated Parmesan cheese

    • Instructions

    1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C).
    2. Cook broccoli according to package instructions. Drain and cut into bite-sized pieces.
    3. In a mixing bowl, combine soup, mayonnaise, and stuffing mix. Fold in broccoli and stir to combine.
    4. Transfer mixture to a greased 1½–2 quart (1.5–2 L) baking dish.
    5. Top evenly with grated cheese.
    6. Bake for 25–30 minutes, until heated through and bubbling.

    Notes

  • Swap in cream of celery or cream of chicken soup if preferred.

    • 4.13 Corn Pudding

    Serves: 6–8  Prep Time: 10 minutes  Bake Time: 1 hour  Total Time: 1 hour 10 minutes

    Description

    A creamy, custardy corn pudding made with canned corn, creamed corn, and eggs. Rich and comforting, this dish lands somewhere between a side and a soft-baked spoonbread. Perfect for holiday tables and Southern-style meals.

    Ingredients

  • 1 (15 oz / 425 g) can whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 (15 oz / 425 g) can creamed corn
  • ½ stick (¼ cup / 57 g) unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup (180 ml) warm milk
  • 4 tbsp (30 g) all-purpose flour
  • 3 tsp (12 g) granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs, beaten

    • Instructions

    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 1½–2 quart (1.5–2 L) casserole dish.
    2. In a saucepan, combine butter and milk and heat gently until butter is melted.
    3. Stir in flour, creamed corn, whole corn, and sugar.
    4. Remove from heat and stir in the beaten eggs. Mix until fully combined.
    5. Pour into the prepared baking dish.
    6. Bake for 1 hour, or until set and lightly golden on top.

    Notes

  • Let cool slightly before serving—it firms as it rests.
  • Excellent alongside ham, roast chicken, or as part of a holiday spread.

    • 4.14 Chipotle Coleslaw

    Serves: 6–8  Prep Time: 20 minutes  Chill Time: 1 hour  Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes

    Description

    A smoky, creamy coleslaw with chipotle-lime dressing. Excellent with grilled meats or spicy mains.

    Ingredients

  • **Dressing**
  • 1½ cups (360 g) mayonnaise (see recipe)
  • 4 tbsp (60 g) chipotle peppers in adobo, minced
  • ⅓ cup (80 ml) fresh lime juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp (6 g) kosher salt
  • ½ tsp (2 g) black pepper
  • **Coleslaw**
  • ½ head purple cabbage, shredded
  • ½ head green cabbage, shredded
  • 2 large or 5 medium carrots, cut into matchsticks
  • ½ cup (30 g) cilantro leaves
  • 3 ears corn, kernels cut off

    • Instructions

    1. Whisk dressing ingredients in a large bowl until smooth.
    2. Shred cabbages and carrots. Blanch corn for 6 minutes, then cool and cut kernels off the cob.
    3. Combine vegetables and cilantro in the bowl with dressing. Toss until evenly coated.
    4. Chill for 1 hour before serving.

    Notes

  • Start with 2 tbsp chipotle and adjust to your preferred spice level.
  • Pairs well with BBQ, pulled pork, or fish tacos.
